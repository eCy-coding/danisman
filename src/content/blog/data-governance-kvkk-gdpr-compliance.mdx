# Data Governance and KVKK/GDPR Compliance: A Corporate Data Strategy Framework

**Category:** AI & Technology | **Reading Time:** 6 min read | **Author:** eCyPro Analiz Ekibi

---

In 2024, Türkiye's Personal Data Protection Authority (Kişisel Verilerin Korunması Kurumu, KVKK — the body charged with enforcing Law No. 6698 on the Protection of Personal Data) imposed 552.67 million Turkish lira in administrative fines following its investigations (KVKK, 2024 Yılı Faaliyet Raporu / 2024 Annual Activity Report). In Europe, GDPR supervisory authorities issued €1.2 billion in fines in the twelve months from 28 January 2024 (DLA Piper GDPR Fines and Data Breach Survey, January 2025). These figures make one point unmistakable: data governance is no longer a narrow compliance exercise confined to the legal department — it now sits at the center of enterprise risk management.

---

## 1. What Is Data Governance?

### 1.1 Definition and Scope

Data governance is the body of policies, processes, standards, and accountabilities through which an organization manages its data as a strategic asset — safeguarding its quality, security, privacy, and effective use (DAMA International, 2017).

Data governance spans three foundational dimensions:

| Dimension | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data Quality** | Accuracy, consistency, completeness, currency |
| **Data Security** | Access control, encryption, breach management |
| **Data Privacy** | KVKK/GDPR compliance, lawful basis for processing |

### 1.2 The Data Governance Maturity Model (DGMM)

eCyPro applies a five-level maturity model:

| Level | Description | Indicator |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **1 — Unaware** | No data policy; ad hoc practices | No data inventory |
| **2 — Reactive** | Crisis-driven data management | Policies limited to specific processes |
| **3 — Proactive** | Defined policies, assigned accountabilities | Data-stewardship roles established |
| **4 — Managed** | Measurement, monitoring, continuous improvement | DQI metrics, audit trails |
| **5 — Optimized** | Data-driven culture, sustained innovation | Monetization of data assets |

## 2. The KVKK Compliance Framework

### 2.1 Core Obligations

Law No. 6698 on the Protection of Personal Data (Kişisel Verilerin Korunması Kanunu, KVKK) entered into force in 2016. Its core obligations include:

**Lawful bases for processing personal data (Articles 5–6):**
- Explicit consent
- A basis expressly provided for by law
- Necessity for the performance of a contract
- A legal obligation binding the data controller
- Legitimate interest (subject to the principle of proportionality)

**Duty to inform (Article 10):** Data subjects must be informed before their personal data is collected.

**Data security obligations (Article 12):** Technical and administrative measures, together with breach-notification duties.

**VERBİS registration:** Registration with the Data Controllers' Registry Information System (Veri Sorumluları Sicil Bilgi Sistemi, VERBİS) is mandatory for data controllers with more than 50 annual employees, or an annual balance-sheet total exceeding 100 million Turkish lira (the financial threshold was raised from 25 million by KVKK Board decision 2023/1154).

### 2.2 The KVKK Compliance Roadmap

**Stage 1 — Personal Data Inventory (Asset Discovery)**

Identify every category of personal data processed within the organization: which data, collected from whom, for what purpose, where it is stored, for how long it is retained, and with whom it is shared.

**Stage 2 — Legal-Basis Analysis**

Establish the specific legal basis under Law No. 6698 for each data-processing activity.

**Stage 3 — Technical and Administrative Measures**

- Data classification and access control
- Encryption (at rest and in transit)
- Logging and monitoring systems
- Employee training
- Policies and procedures

**Stage 4 — VERBİS Registration and Privacy Policies**

Record data-processing activities in the Personal Data Inventory and subsequently register them with VERBİS.

**Stage 5 — Continuity**

Annual compliance review, breach-response drills, and ongoing monitoring of regulatory developments.

## 3. GDPR Compliance: Critical Considerations for Turkish Organizations

### 3.1 KVKK Compared with the GDPR

| Criterion | KVKK | GDPR |
|-----------|------|------|
| **Territorial scope** | Data controllers operating in Türkiye | Worldwide, wherever the personal data of EU residents is processed |
| **Data-breach notification** | Notification to the KVKK Board without undue delay (the notification deadline is less rigidly codified than under the GDPR) | To the supervisory authority within 72 hours |
| **Data protection officer** | Not mandatory (recommended for certain categories of controller) | Mandatory for certain categories of controller (DPO) |
| **Cookie compliance** | Required under the relevant Board decisions | Active consent required (in conjunction with the ePrivacy framework) |
| **Maximum penalty** | Fixed administrative fine bands set out in Law No. 6698, Art. 18 | Up to 4% of annual global turnover or €20 million, whichever is greater |

### 3.2 Priorities for Turkish Organizations Active in the EU Market

- **The GDPR's extraterritorial reach:** Turkish organizations that offer goods or services to EU residents, or that monitor their behavior, fall within the scope of the GDPR regardless of where the organization itself is established.
- **Data-transfer mechanisms:** Transfers of personal data from the EU to Türkiye require Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or binding corporate rules (BCRs).
- **Cookie-policy alignment:** Websites serving EU visitors require GDPR-compliant cookie-consent mechanisms.

## 4. Data Security Governance: ISO 27001

### 4.1 The ISO 27001 Framework

ISO/IEC 27001 is the international standard for an Information Security Management System (ISMS). It forms the technical foundation of privacy compliance and safeguards three core principles:

- **Confidentiality:** Preventing unauthorized access
- **Integrity:** Preserving the accuracy of data
- **Availability:** Ensuring authorized users have uninterrupted access to data

### 4.2 Critical Controls

**Access management:** The principle of least privilege, periodic reviews of access rights, and the immediate deactivation of former employees' accounts.

**Encryption:** Both at-rest and in-transit encryption are required for sensitive data categories.

**Backup and business continuity:** The 3-2-1 backup rule — three copies, on two different media, with one copy stored off-site.

**Breach-response planning:** Simulated data-breach exercises and annual drills.

## 5. Artificial Intelligence and Data Governance

The enterprise adoption of generative AI tools adds new dimensions to data governance:

- **Training-data privacy:** Corporate data transferred to large language models (LLMs) requires a data-processing agreement.
- **Output auditing:** Verifying whether AI-generated output inadvertently contains personal data.
- **Model security:** Access control and audit trails for enterprise LLM deployments.
- **The EU Artificial Intelligence Act (AI Act):** Additional obligations for high-risk AI systems, phasing in from 2026 onward.
