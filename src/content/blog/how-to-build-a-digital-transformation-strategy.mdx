# How to Build a Digital Transformation Strategy?

Companies across Türkiye and beyond are investing heavily in digital transformation initiatives, yet by most estimates only a minority deliver the results their sponsors expected. What separates the two groups is rarely the technology stack — it is the **strategy** governing its adoption.

This guide sets out seven proven steps for building a digital transformation strategy at the corporate level.

## Why Does Digital Transformation Fail?

McKinsey has found that roughly **70%** of large-scale transformations fall short of their objectives — and the decisive obstacles are organizational rather than technological. The factors that recur most often are:

- **Lack of leadership commitment**
- **Talent and capability gaps**
- **Organizational resistance**

Technology ranks last among the causes of failure. Before asking "which software should we buy?", the organization must first answer a prior question: "why are we transforming at all?"

## A Seven-Step Framework for Digital Transformation Strategy

### Step 1: Strategic Diagnosis — Map the Current State

Begin with a Digital Maturity Assessment. It should measure five dimensions:

| Dimension | Guiding Question | Metric |
|-------|------|--------|
| Customer Experience | Is the customer journey digital? | NPS, CES |
| Operations | What share of processes is automated? | % manual work |
| Data | Are decisions data-driven? | Decision-cycle duration |
| Technology | Is system integration adequate? | API maturity |
| Talent | What is the level of digital capability? | Training investment |

### Step 2: Define the Vision and KPIs

A digital transformation strategy must be anchored to measurable targets. Apply the SMART framework:

```
Poor: "We will digitalize our operations."
Good: "We will reduce the order-to-delivery cycle from 15 days to 3 days within 24 months."
```

**Recommended KPI set:**
- Operational efficiency gain (%)
- Share of revenue from digital channels (%)
- Customer satisfaction (NPS delta)
- Change in the IT-to-business cost ratio
- Employee digital-capability score

### Step 3: The Roadmap — The Three-Horizons Model

McKinsey's Three Horizons model balances short- and long-term initiatives:

**Horizon 1 (0–12 months) — Strengthen the core:**
- Automate existing processes
- Clean up the data infrastructure
- Establish critical integrations

**Horizon 2 (12–36 months) — Expand the business model:**
- Develop new digital products and services
- Renew customer segments
- Build ecosystem partnerships

**Horizon 3 (36+ months) — Transformative growth:**
- Embed artificial intelligence into core processes
- Pursue platformization and network effects
- Design new business-model architectures

### Step 4: Governance Structure

Digital transformation is not an "IT project" — it is a corporate strategic initiative, and its governance should reflect that standing.

**Recommended structure:**
- **Steering Committee:** CEO and the C-suite, meeting monthly
- **Transformation Office (TO):** a Transformation Lead plus domain champions
- **Agile Squads:** mixed business-and-IT teams working in two-week sprint cycles

### Step 5: Technology Selection — A "Platform, Not Tool" Approach

Technology decisions should follow strategy, not precede it. Selection criteria include:

1. **Integration capability:** API compatibility with existing systems
2. **Scalability:** Can the platform accommodate future growth scenarios?
3. **Local data sovereignty:** compliance with KVKK (Türkiye's Personal Data Protection Law, Law No. 6698) and in-country data storage
4. **Total cost of ownership (TCO):** a five-year projection
5. **Vendor risk:** the supplier's financial health and reference base

### Step 6: Change Management — Put People at the Center

The largest obstacle to any digital transformation is the human factor. A well-established approach is the ADKAR model:

**The ADKAR model:**
- **A**wareness: why is the organization changing? (communication)
- **D**esire: motivation for individual participation
- **K**nowledge: training and skills development
- **A**bility: the opportunity and support to apply new skills
- **R**einforcement: recognizing and sustaining early wins

### Step 7: The Measurement-and-Adaptation Cycle

A strategy is not a static document — it is a living cycle.

```
Plan → Execute → Measure → Learn → Adapt → Plan
(a continuous, adaptive planning loop)
```

**Monthly review template:**
- Update the KPI dashboard (actual vs. target)
- List obstacles and risks
- Revise the priority ranking
- Identify quick wins
