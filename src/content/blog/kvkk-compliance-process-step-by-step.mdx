Law No. 6698 on the Protection of Personal Data — KVKK, from the Turkish *Kişisel Verilerin Korunması Kanunu*, and the country's principal data-protection statute — entered into force in 2016. As of 2026, a substantial share of Turkish organizations remain stalled at the "we should get started" stage. This guide sets out the practical, sequential steps an organization beginning its compliance program from a standing start should follow.

> **Legal notice:** This content is provided for general information only and does not constitute legal advice. For guidance specific to your organization, consult a licensed attorney or a qualified KVKK compliance advisor.

## Why Must an Organization Comply with KVKK?

**Administrative fines.** The Personal Data Protection Board (Kurul) — KVKK's supervisory authority — may impose administrative fines under Article 18, denominated in Turkish lira (TL). These amounts are revalued at the start of each year in line with the statutory revaluation rate, so the operative figures rise annually; the 2026 ranges are:

- Failure to fulfill the transparency (disclosure) obligation: TL 85,437–1,709,200
- Failure to fulfill data-security obligations, including breach notification: TL 256,357–17,092,242
- Non-compliance with a Board decision: TL 427,263–17,092,242
- Failure to notify the Board of the standard contract used for a cross-border transfer (a fine category added by Law No. 7499 in 2024): TL 90,308–1,806,177

**Criminal exposure.** Beyond these administrative fines, conduct such as the unlawful obtaining, disclosure, or retention of personal data can also trigger criminal liability for individual officers under general Turkish criminal law. Organizations should treat this as a standing exposure for decision-makers rather than a novelty, and should confirm the current statutory position with counsel before communicating specifics to a board.

**Competitive standing.** KVKK compliance functions as a credibility signal, particularly in B2B relationships and cross-border partnerships, where counterparties increasingly treat data-governance maturity as a precondition for doing business.

## The Seven-Step KVKK Compliance Process

### Step 1: Building the Data Inventory

The first — and most consequential — step is descriptive: which categories of personal data does the organization process, where are they held, who processes them, and for what purpose?

**Categories to interrogate:**
- Customer data (name, contact details, transaction history)
- Employee data (payroll, personnel file, health records)
- Supplier and business-partner data
- Website visitor data (cookies, IP addresses)
- CCTV camera footage

**Tooling:** a spreadsheet-based data inventory, or dedicated data-governance software (e.g., OneTrust, Usercentrics).

### Step 2: Establishing the Lawful Basis

Every processing activity must rest on one of the lawful bases enumerated in KVKK Articles 5 and 6:

| Lawful Basis | Typical Use |
|---------------|----------------|
| Explicit consent | Marketing emails, profiling |
| Performance of a contract | Delivery address, invoicing details |
| Legal obligation | Filings with the Social Security Institution (SGK), tax records |
| Legitimate interest | Fraud prevention, security |
| Vital interest | Medical emergencies |

### Step 3: Drafting Privacy Notices

A privacy notice — the *aydınlatma metni*, or KVKK Article 10 disclosure text — is mandatory at every point personal data is collected:
- General website privacy notice
- Contact-form privacy notice
- Employee privacy notice
- CCTV privacy notice
- Customer/supplier contract addendum

**Drafting standard:** plain, comprehensible language. Legal terminology must not stand between the data subject and their ability to understand how their data is used.

### Step 4: VERBİS Registration

VERBİS — the *Veri Sorumluları Sicil Bilgi Sistemi*, or Data Controllers' Registry Information System — is the central registry through which controllers declare their processing activities to the Board.

**Who must register:**
- Natural- or legal-person data controllers whose annual headcount exceeds 50 employees, OR whose annual balance-sheet total exceeds TL 100 million (the balance-sheet threshold was raised from TL 25 million by Board decision 2023/1154)
- The Türkiye representative of a foreign-headquartered company

**Registration portal:** verbis.kvkk.gov.tr

**Declared content:** data categories, processing purposes, retention periods, recipient parties, and security measures.

### Step 5: Technical and Administrative Security Measures

**Technical:**
- Access control (RBAC — Role-Based Access Control)
- Encryption (data at rest and in transit)
- Log management (who accessed which data, and when)
- Penetration testing (at least annually)
- A backup policy

**Administrative:**
- KVKK awareness training (all personnel, annually)
- Data-processing agreements with every data processor
- A breach-response plan, including the 72-hour notification obligation

### Step 6: The Data-Subject Application Mechanism

Under KVKK Article 11, data subjects may exercise the following rights:
- The right to learn whether their personal data is being processed
- The right to request information where their data has been processed
- The right to learn the purpose of processing and whether the data is used accordingly
- The right to know the third parties, at home or abroad, to whom the data has been transferred
- The right to rectification of incomplete or inaccurate data
- The right to erasure or destruction of their data
- The right to object to an adverse outcome produced solely by automated processing
- The right to claim compensation for damage arising from unlawful processing

**Mandatory elements:** a formal application form, a 30-day response window, and an application channel accessible at all times.

### Step 7: Continuity and Governance

KVKK compliance is a standing process, not a one-off project.

**Governance structure:**
- **Data Protection Officer (DPO):** not a strict legal requirement under KVKK, but of material importance in practice — particularly for larger organizations and those handling high volumes of sensitive (special-category) data.
- **Annual compliance review.**
- **Incident-response drills** (once a year).
- **Independent third-party audit** (every two to three years).

## Common KVKK Mistakes

1. **"We put a notice on the site — done."** A privacy notice is invalid if its content is inaccurate or does not correspond to the actual processing it purports to describe.
2. **Scoping errors.** Omitting supplier and third-party data from the inventory is a recurring failure mode.
3. **Poorly designed consent mechanisms.** Pre-checked boxes and "implied consent" do not satisfy the explicit-consent standard.
4. **Delayed breach notification.** The 72-hour notification window is frequently missed.
5. **Incomplete or inaccurate VERBİS entries.** Board inspections routinely cross-check the VERBİS registration record against actual practice.
