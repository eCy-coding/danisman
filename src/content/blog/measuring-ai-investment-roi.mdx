# Measuring AI Investment ROI: The CFO Framework

Measuring the return on AI investments requires a fundamentally different approach from traditional IT projects. Many CFOs should ask not "how much does AI earn?" but first "how much loss does it prevent?"

## Why Traditional ROI Falls Short

Classic NPV calculations assume linear cash flows. But AI:

- **Compounds**: It learns from data and improves over time.
- **Creates cross-functional value**: A single model impacts dozens of business processes.
- **Carries defensive value**: It protects against competitive disruption.

## Three-Layer Value Framework

### 1. Direct Savings

Workforce productivity, error reduction, process acceleration:

- **Example:** AI-assisted claims processing at an insurer → processing time from 8 days to 14 hours → €4.2M annual operational savings.

### 2. Revenue Growth

Personalized experiences, improved customer acquisition:

- **Example:** E-commerce AI recommendation → conversion rate 2.1% → 3.8% → net incremental revenue +€8.7M annually.

### 3. Risk Mitigation (Optionality Value)

Regulatory penalty risk, cybersecurity, reputational loss:

> "The risk prevention value of AI is often 2-3× greater than direct benefits. This is almost invisible in standard ROI calculations." — eCyPro AI Valuation Report 2026

## Practical Calculation Steps

1. **Baseline Definition:** Clarify current-state metrics (error rate, cycle time, CAC).
2. **Scenario Modelling:** Best / base / worst case projections.
3. **Software + Integration Costs:** Licensing, infrastructure, internal training, change management.
4. **Measurement System:** Which metrics, which tools, what cadence?
5. **Internal Ownership:** Finance + IT + Business unit must agree on shared KPIs.
